Chest and upper back Includes upper and lower back, male breast area and nipples

Choose the one symptom that's bothering you most today.

  • Breathing problems

    Wheezing, panting or feeling out of breath. Difficult or noisy breathing.

  • Cough

    Hacking, tickly or dry cough, without other cold symptoms. Coughing up phlegm or mucus.

  • Coughing or vomiting up blood

    Coughing up blood in spit (vomit), phlegm or mucus.

  • Hiccups

    Hiccups, with possible fever. Hiccups won't stop.

  • Palpitations

    Feeling the heart is beating too fast or too slow. Pounding or irregular heartbeat. Racing heart, fluttering in the chest, skipped beats.

Chest, breasts and upper back

  • Chest (male breast) and nipple problems

    Lumps, pain or any worries about skin changes. Nipple discharge or nipple turning inward.

  • Chest or upper back injury

    An injury to the chest or upper back on/in the last 7 days, that has not cut the skin. Broken, painful or bruised ribs. Injuries from the collarbone to bottom of the ribcage.

  • Chest or upper back injury with a cut or wound

    An injury to the chest or upper back on/in the last 7 days. Broken, painful or bruised ribs with a wound.

  • Chest pain

    Any chest or upper back pain. Chest pain with or without arm, shoulder neck or jaw pain. Tightness in the chest.

Lower back

  • Lower back injury

    An injury to the lower back (below the rib cage) or bottom in the last 7 days that has not cut the skin.

  • Lower back injury with a cut or wound

    An injury to the lower back (below the rib cage) or bottom in the last 7 days with a cut or wound.

  • Lower back pain

    Pain or ache in the lower part of the back.
